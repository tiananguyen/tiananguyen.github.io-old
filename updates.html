Motivation Vs. Discipline

April 9, 2018

Recently I’ve been hit by a sudden shock and turn of events. But that’s a story I’ll have to talk about next time. Instead, let me share you what I have learned from the experience so far.

By the way I see it, if you want to get something done, there are two ways to make this happen. The first one is the most popular option : motivating yourself. Motivation, as defined by the Oxford Dictionary, is the reason or reasons for acting or behaving in a particular way. The second one is one that I’ve neglected for a while : discipling yourself. Discipline, as defined by the Oxford Dictionary is the practice of training people to obey rules or a code of behaviour, using punishment to correct disobedience. Although both of these terms seem to almost be the same, there is one big difference that I found about the two. That is, motivation relies on your mood. If for some reason, one day you don’t feel happy, there is a higher chance of you not being productive because you’ve become dependent on motivation to get you through work. In contrast, if you begin to discipline yourself to work, you’ll always be able to work no matter how you’re feeling that day. To put all of this in simpler terms, if you were an olympian, you wouldn’t want to wait until you’re in olympic form to start training.

Error 404

March 23, 2018

Error 404. Page Not Found. We’ve all seen this before. Whether it’s because out internet isn’t working at the moment, or it’s because the webmaster did something wrong and it fixing it at the moment, there are so many possibilities of why this page pops up on our screen. One of the biggest causes behind this error page is due to cybercrimes. As more individuals and items connect to the internet, there has been more cybercrimes and hacks occurring that need to be taken care of with the help of cybersecurity. Ultimately, we need to make sure technology is being used for good and not being misused for harm. In the films I,Robot and Minority Report, it is clearly demonstrated that cybersecurity is necessary, for individuals in the films take advantage and hack into the software and hardware of artificial intelligence and system. Success of companies now rely on the digital economy, which has led to cybersecurity becoming more and more important as social obligation to ensure security is forefronting our minds when we go about our day to day lives. This ultimately leads to the question : How can we make cybersecurity better and more effective

To start off, let’s dive more into how cybersecurity even came about. As scientific discovery grew, scientists needed better developed technology to store their data and share it with others. In order for scientists to share this information with one another, computer networking was created. This was system in which multiple computers(each computer referred to as a node) are connected to each other to share resources from one computer to another. As this system developed, it soon transformed into the World Wide Web, which permitted individuals to quickly and efficiently share information with one another from around the world. In 1985, the global Internet increased in Europe and the invention of the Domain Name System(DNS) came about. The Domain Name System has become an essential component to the World Wide Web and Internet in general, for it assigns domain names to Internet resources. This ultimately made connecting things to the internet easier, which resulted in the numerous Internet of Things(IoT) today. Because we are now able to connect so many things to the internet, there are many benefits to doing so, such as having access to others’ locations in case they are in danger and creating success for companies, since they rely on digital economy. However, there are numerous disadvantages that we need to take care of as we connect more items to the internet. This is where cybersecurity kicks in. In the past— and present — individuals are using the World Wide Web to scam other people through the Domain Naming System with countries like China tracking popular domains until they scam individuals. However, this is only one of the same instances in which technology creates harm and we are in need of cybersecurity. With more efficient cybersecurity, we’ll be able to keep computer systems running smoothly and prevent attacks, since cybersecurity is essentially a group of technologies and processes designed to protect networks and data from attack, damage or unauthorized access.

In recent years, cities are becoming smarter and smarter and communities around the world, from small towns to big metropolitan areas, are turning to modern technologies to connect government agencies and citizens to deal with urban problems such as traffic congestion, public service shortcomings, and energy shortages. Because smart cities rely on wireless and mobile technologies, a smart city ecosystem is a widely interconnected network, much bigger than any regular system of a private organization such as a business. With such interconnection and availability, it is hard to know what is exposed and the level of exposure. Attackers have many potential ways to interfere with the services. Zhen Li and Qi Liao further explore this danger, in their article “Economic Solutions to Improve Cybersecurity of governments and smart cities via vulnerability markets.” After concluding that any technological products would be securer if they are produced with higher level of security, or found vulnerabilities are patched by vendors before the information is exploited by attackers, Li and Liao proposed two alternative economic solutions the government may use to address the cybersecurity challenges facing smart cities and e-government. The first one was the creation of incentive systems for vendors to invest in security. Through this process, it would hopefully further motivate vendors to get cybersecurity so that they can get government company benefits. The second proposal they developed was a vulnerability market for defensive buyers to acquire vulnerabilities to prevent the vulnerability information from falling into the wrong hands.

As the success of companies rely on digital economy, companies have been collecting an abundant amount of data from individuals about their personal information. While individuals should have the right to know what data is being collected from them and what happens after the collection, this is not the case. Even when there is a cybercrime and companies are in a data breach, there is a lot of hesitation that comes about about multiple things. The first topic of discussion is the amount of disclosure the company should inform the public. Because companies sometimes don’t understand what exactly went on in the breach, there is an uncertainty about when to disclose information and how long to withhold it. However, companies have to also take into consideration how individuals being attacked will feel, for the company should be transparent to the world in order to gain trust, but they don’t want everyone to always know every single thing that has happened at the same time. This leads to the next ethical issue of trust. Building trust between the company and customer is extremely important, which requires two roles for the company : roles as a security and role as a trust. Since there is a grey area of performing harm assessment that doesn’t only meet the minimum requirement, companies must foster a greater trust in their brand and be transparent at a moderate amount. This leads to one of the hardest ethical issues : the potential of hard to come from the release of information. It’s important to have a commitment to transparency, for companies have responsibility to holder of the data to do the right thing. Companies need to put themselves in position of the victim and go about the best way as to how the victim will receive information of an event that occurred.

Ultimately, cybersecurity is an essential commodity of the future that will not only protect individuals from getting their personal information stolen, but will also further the economy. However, to get to a good cybersecurity system, we must start from the ourselves, and from there we will be able to spread and reach towards others. Since the concept of cybersecurity is often not easy to explain, for it concerns both humans and systems, we must know what cybersecurity truly is ourselves and acknowledge that cybersecurity is a problem we shouldn’t look over. Without understanding this part, there would be a lack of sense of urgency in many individuals which results in no action towards a better cybersecurity system. From here, we can go to companies— or if ambitious enough, make a company ourselves— of how important cybersecurity is to our lives. There needs to be some kinds of social obligation in the future to ensure security is forefronting our minds when we go about our day to day lives.

Internet of Toys

November 5, 2017

From the first phone call through a vertical metal cone in 1876, to the cutting edge smartphones of 2017 that can detect your face to unlock it, technology has immensely changed throughout the years. It is that change—that innovation and ability to evolve— that makes technology so amazing. Everyday, individuals are working to create new advancements that will help our community and society as a whole. Due to the constant change in technology, we are now able to do things instantaneously and easily. However, as insignificant as the first computer may seem in comparison to the lightweight laptops individuals own today, every new invention and creation has made a tremendous impact in our lives. I had no clue how harmful the internet could be to our lives until I attended various cybersecurity talks last month at Santa Clara University due to the fact that it was cyber security month. It became clear that everyone should be knowledgable about these harmful things in life in order for individuals to be mindful of everything they do. But before I dive into the harm, I'll quickly outline the background of the internet.

As computer networking further developed and grew, British Scientist, Tim Berners-Lee, quickly realized that he can develop an internet application built on the physical capabilities of computer networking. In 1989, Berners-Lee took his vision of a global hyperlinked information system and brought it to life through the inventions of the World Wide Web(WWW), which permitted individuals to quickly and efficiently share information with one another from around the world. However, Berners-Lee has to take a series of steps before he could get to this point. In 1985, the global Internet increased in Europe and the invention of the Domain Name System(DNS) came about. The Domain Name System has become an essential component to the World Wide Web and Internet in general, for it assigns domain names to Internet resources. Another important aspect to the Internet was also built upon the Domain Name System, called the Uniform Resource Locator(URL), which specified the location of a web resource on a computer network and the device retrieving it. From here, Berners-Lee’s vision for hypertext links to include multimedia documents was born. Instead of having screens filled with texts of one font and one size, Berners-Lee used his development of Hypertext Transfer Protocol(HTTP) and his development of the language Hypertext Markup Language(HTML) to make the Graphic User Interface more appealing to look at and added media to his project. The first website in the world was finally developed and hosted on Berners-Lee’s NeXT computer, as shown in the image to the left. Through this first website, Berners-Lee explained the basic features of the web and gave instructions on how to access other individuals’ information, to further help scientists around the world. However, as this phenomenon spread around the world, it became popular and on demand among everyone across the globe. As a result, on April 30, 1993, CERN put the World Wide Web software in a public domain and ultimately declared that this World Wide Web technology would be freely accessible by anyone. From there, the technology began to be widely used, which greatly helped everyone worldwide.

Although computer networking and the World Wide Web are helpful to everyone across the globe, it has caused harm and issues for our society. While the Internet of Things(the concept of connecting a device with an on/off switch to the internet) grew, more and more cyber crimes have come about, for people use the World Wide Web to scam others. Unfortunately, we can find harm anywhere— even in children play toys. As trivial as a child’s toy may seem, if it is connected smart, interactive, and internet-connected, the whole family could be unsafe. Since these toys contain sensors, microphones, cameras, and other multimedia capabilities, the interactions or conversations between the toy and the child has to be collected somewhere. While they are often sent and stored by the manufacturer or developer through cloud service, some third party companies managing the voice recognition software used in the toys collect information instead. This leaves voice recordings, home addresses, and other personal data vulnerable and exposed if the security of the data is not sufficiently protected. Even when one may think that one are too far away for the toy to detect one’s voice, it is possible for toys with microphones to record and collect conversations within earshot of the object. This easily leaks any information about the child’s name, school, and activities to the public and could potentially use this for identity fraud. Connection to the internet also offers criminals a way to misuse sensitive data, like GPS location information.

Ultimately, anything that can be connected to the internet, will be connected. Eventually, the use of internet will dominate our world, which will not only impact how we work, but how we live as well. As a result, it’s important and imperative that we pay careful attention to what we do with our access to the internet. Not only will this extra caution of our actions help us be protected in the long term, but it will help our society as a whole.

Eliminate Week

May 19, 2017

Maternal Neonatal Tetanus. That is what we are fighting against. Every year Key Club International hosts an Eliminate Week- a week in which Key Clubbers from around the world raise awareness and funds for the Eliminate Project. For those of you who don't know about the Elimiante Project, it is a project dedicated towards eliminating maternal and neonatal tetanus (MNT)- a disease that affects women and newborns around the world. Just $1.80 can save a mother and her child from the effects. With just 18 countries affected left, Key Club International and UNICEF are more determined than ever to work together to fulfill their pledges to eliminate this disease. This past week was filled with fun spirit days that reminded me and many others why the Eliminate Project is so important.

MONDAY : Mommy Monday. It was time to bring a stuffed animal to school! This was the day that probably raised the most awareness for the Eliminate Project. As I walked around the hall with my moose stuffed animal that I like to call Moosey, everyone always petted and commented on it. The most common thing I heard was, "It's such a strange and random day to bring a stuffed animal to school, but I guess it's Tiana so..." At least I could raise awareness by telling them the reason for being weird and more about the Eliminate Project!

TUESDAY : Cue The Blue. Cue the Blue Tuesday meant that it was time to look through my closet and bleed blue! After the Key Club Meeting on Wednesday, there was an ELiminate themed backdrop set up outside that all the members took photos with and posted on social media to complete the challenge.
