Computer History Museum : Design_Code_Build


As an instructor at the Computer History Museum's program, Design_Code_Build, I introduce groups of students to concepts of computer programming by working with Raspberry Pi technology and Python. Aside from teaching, I also guide the students through activities that focus on improving the their problem solving and innovation skills, and a tour of the museum to help them make personal connections with artifacts to inspire students to learn from history to design the future.

In addition to teaching the students programming and about the history of computing, we also host special days in which we teach educators— whether they are in history, maker spaces, digital technology, and more— the skills we introduce to students. On top of that, we also help brainstorm ideas on how to get the youth more excited and involved in computing as well as how to incorporate all the information learned into their own program or curriculum.
